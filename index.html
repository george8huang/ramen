What is Ramen?

Before we get into it, we should consider: what counts as ramen? Ramen is a Japanese noodle dish which comprises of five core ingredients:

Broth

Sauce

Noodles

Toppings

Oil

Click on an ingredient to learn more!

Broth

This is the soup and foundation of ramen. Base ingredients include chicken, pork, seafood, and vegetables. Clear soups, found in shio and shoyu ramen, are created in low temperatures, while "white soups", found in tonkotsu and tori, are created in high temperatures.

Sauce

Sauce provides the salt and umami for the broth. Common ingredients include soy sauce, wine, sake, kelp, sardines, miso, and salt. Ultimately, the sauce is what provides the depth of flavor we enjoy in a good bowl of ramen.

Noodles

Noodles are made from water and wheat, which creates the noodle shape, and alkaline water, which gives it texture and color. The balance of these ingredients must be perfect for good ramen, which is why many restaurants prefer to buy their noodles rather than take on the challenge.

Toppings

Toppings give each bowl of ramen a delightful set of accents to help it appeal to any kind of customer. Common toppings include chashu, green onions, bamboo shoots, seaweed, and a marinated egg.

Oil

On its own, oil doesn't seem like the most important or appealing aspect, but it's actually quite necessary. Oil helps elevate all the other flavors in the broth. In addition, it gives the noodles a smooth, slippery texture and keeps the ramen warm for us.
